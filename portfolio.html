Welcome to my Digital Portfolio!

Here you can view a series of art pieces all created around the brief of decay and learn about my reasoning and inspiration behind each piece. These pieces will cover my thoughts and feelings about myself, my social/medical transition and experiences with love. As well as showcasing some smaller pieces, giving a view into the experimentation I take part in between larger more meaningful pieces.

To Meet The Standards
Pastels on Paper
A3 2023
A piece about body dysphoria and camera distortion.

"It's so hard to depict a body I don't like. My only references are photos from my phone, they're inaccurate. To meet the standards of a perfect reflection. It almost seems impossible. This one is good though..."

Self Portrait 1
Charcoal on Canvas
A4 2023
Originally when this piece was first being created it was destined to be chopped and sliced up for a collage...

But. As I continued to work on the piece I started to realise not only how much I love the texture of charcoal on canvas, but I also noticed my own opinion on my face change drastically from negative to positive. While it may only be a simple self portrait I don't think id have the more positive self image I have today writing this. If I had not created it.

This art is a part of me now, my transition, it can never be removed.

Self Improvement
Charcoal on Watercolour Paper
A3 2023
For another art piece I decided to move onto another insecurity of mine. I have a problem with my ribs, caused by me wearing ill-fitting bras and sleeping in them early transition. This has caused one half of my ribs to jut out slightly further than the other half. I had a lot of ideas surrounding this piece, eventually deciding to use my ribs as a representation of how I don't fit in with societies norms.

"Would I have this self hate if there was nothing to conform to?

Would I be happy with myself?

I don't know if I can tell what "improvements" are forced or of my own volition"

Life Drawing 2
Charcoal on Paper
A3 2023
Another one of my life drawing pieces done this year.

A life drawing piece depicting a man resting on a metal pole, the same pole shown in the previous life drawing piece.
More emphasis and time was spent on capturing the lighting of the scene within the piece and how it interacts with the figure.

Recluse
Charcoal on Paper
A3 2023
The third and final life drawing piece within this progression log.
With this art piece, in my opinion, being the strongest of the three.

This piece relies less on details and experimentation with light and shadow, instead opting to let the posing of the figures and more stylized nature of the piece stand out as a more striking approach than the other life drawing pieces.

The choices of poses in this piece are also deliberate, with one being closed off, protecting its torso and blocking you off with its forearm, and the other pose being vulnerable and open. Its torso unprotected, arms locked together above its head.
This helps to bring juxtaposition to the piece using just body language.

What A Fool
Charcoal on Canvas
A4 2023
A piece showing my feelings about a recent romantic experience I missed.

For this artwork I wanted to incorporate the feelings around missing that chance, I also decided to simplify my own portrayal in this piece, I am not trans, I am not neurodivergant, I am just a women, wanting another.

This decision ended up turning into the idea that i was to depict myself as a stereotypical female stick figure, probably the most basic depiction of a women I can think of, making the representation of gender in the piece more binary, more relatable.

The place depicted in the art is where I found out that I had missed that romantic experience.

Splits//Don't Look Away Now
Charcoal on Canvas
75 x 80 cm 2024
A piece about my ongoing transition, stuck between male and female, both sides pulling me equally.

This piece depicts my body at the time of creating it, my breast development just starting, my flared ribs out in the open, body hair, it is unapologetic not only to myself, but to all the viewers.

Don't look away now, feel my shame.

This piece is also my peak so far in terms of shading and colour, colour especially being something absent from the majority of my pieces this year. It's used to show a sense of hope, of finality, that I am over the hill that is starting hormones, legally I am Sadie, and soon my physical form shall match my name.
