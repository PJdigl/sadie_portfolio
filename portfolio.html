Ribs
Charcoal on Watercolour Paper
A3 2023

After completing (and wowing myself) with the self-portrait, I decided to move onto another piece about another insecurity of mine. I have a problem with my ribs, caused by me wearing ill-fitting bras and sleeping in them early transition. This has caused one half of my ribs to jut out slightly further than the other half. I had a lot of ideas surrounding this piece, shown in the sketches on this slide, but as you'll see, I eventually settled on something a bit more grounded in reality.

Life Drawing 2
Charcoal on Paper
A3 2023

Another one of my life drawing peices done this year.

A life drawing peice depicting a man resting on a metal pole, the same pole shown in the previous life drawing peice.
More emphisis and time was spent on capturing the lighting of the scene within the peice and how it interacts with the figure.

Notice the level of shading especially around the upper half of the body and the stomach, with thicker more abrasive lines used for the darker areas in most places.

Recluse
Charcoal on Paper
A3 2023

The third and final life drawing piece within this progression log.
With this art piece, in my opinion, being the strongest of the three.

This piece relies less on details and experimentation with light and shadow, instead opting to let the posing of the figures and more stylized nature of the piece stand out as a more striking approach than the other life drawing pieces.

The choices of poses in this piece are also deliberate, with one being closed off, protecting its torso and blocking you off with its forearm, and the other pose being vulnerable and open. Its torso unprotected, arms locked together above its head.
This helps to bring juxtaposition to the piece using just body language.

What A Fool
Charcoal on Canvas
A4 2023

A piece showing my feelings about a recent romantic experience I missed.

For this piece I wanted to incorporate the feelings around missing that chance into some of my art, I also decided to simplify my own protrayal in these peices, I am not trans, I am not neurodivergant, I am just a women, wanting another. My solution was to depict myself as a steriotypical female stick figures, probably the most basic depiction of a women I can think of, making the representation of gender in the piece more binary, more relatable.
